The five kingdoms of living organisms

  1. Bacteria
    • Single-celled organisms that lack a nucleus.
    • Classified as prokaryotes.
  2. Protists
    • Single-celled organisms without a rigid cell wall.
    • Have a well-defined nucleus and are classified as eukaryotes.
  3. Plants
    • Multicellular organisms capable of photosynthesis.
    • Incapable of active movement and have a rigid cell wall.
  4. Fungi
    • Unable to perform photosynthesis, leading a sedentary lifestyle and feeding by decomposing organic matter.
    • Single-celled or multicellular organisms.
  5. Animals
    • Unable to perform photosynthesis.
    • Capable of active movement and obtain food from plants or other animals.
